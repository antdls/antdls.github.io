The Petit Palais is a 19th/20th century art museum, built for the Exposition Universelle of 1900. Its administration was looking for a way to get visitors interested in the building as much as in the works of art.

We designed a type of audioguide, a pod containing a "piece of memory" from someone visiting the building on its inauguration. In concrete terms, it is a box, containing a speaker and animated lights, which "speaks" with information points in the museum. The box represents a person speaking to various other characters such as the building's architect, a construction worker, a painter, and so on. These characters inform the visitors about interesting aspects of the building.

The main idea is to get visitors to look for these hidden information points by wandering around the building and searching for interesting parts of the architecture independently. {% include gallery %}
