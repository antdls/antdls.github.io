The Petit Palais is a 19th/20th century art museum, built for the 1900 World's Fair. Its administration was looking for a way to get visitors interested in the building as much as in the works of art.

We designed a kind of audioguide, a pod containing a piece of memory from someone's visit of the building during its inauguration. In concrete terms, it is a box, containing a speaker and animated lights, which "speaks" with information points in the museum. It represents someone, the box, speaking to various other characters such as the building's architect, a construction worker, a painter, and so on. These characters will inform the visitors about interesting aspects of the building.

The main idea is to get visitors to look for these hidden information points, wandering around the building and looking by themselves for interesting things in the architecture. {% include gallery %}
